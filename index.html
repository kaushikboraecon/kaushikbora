About

I am an Agricultural Economist and Policy Researcher at the Borlaug Institute for South Asia, International Maize and Wheat Improvement Center (BISA-CIMMYT), New Delhi, where I work on the Atlas of Climatic Adaptation in South Asian Agriculture (ACASA) and Bundled Crop Insurance. My work in the project integrates spatial analysis, high-resolution gridded datasets, and mixed-methods approaches to assess climatic risks and evaluate adaptation strategies in agriculture.

My research spans agricultural economics, impact evaluation, gender-responsive technology assessment, and spatial prioritization, with a focus on South Asian Agriculture.

Previously, I was a Visiting Scientist at the Evans School of Policy Analysis and Research (EPAR), University of Washington, and a Research Fellow at the Indian Statistical Institute, Bangalore, where I completed my doctoral research on the effects of climatic extremes on fertilizer use in Indian agriculture.
